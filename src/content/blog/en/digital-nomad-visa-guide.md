## Introduction

Over the past few years, remote work has transformed how people live and work. Millions of professionals are no longer tied to a single office or city, and many are choosing to combine remote careers with international travel.

As a result, governments around the world have started introducing **digital nomad visas**, special residency permits designed for remote workers who earn income from abroad.

These visas allow location-independent professionals to legally live in another country while continuing to work for foreign companies or clients.

According to research from [MBO Partners](https://www.mbopartners.com/state-of-independence/digital-nomads/), millions of professionals worldwide now identify as digital nomads, and the number continues to grow as remote work becomes more mainstream.

## What is a digital nomad visa?

A **digital nomad visa** is a type of residence permit that allows remote workers to live in a country while working for companies or clients located elsewhere.

Unlike traditional work visas, digital nomad visas usually require applicants to:

- work remotely for foreign companies or clients
- prove a minimum monthly income
- have health insurance coverage
- avoid working for local employers

The goal of these visas is to attract remote professionals who can contribute to the local economy without competing for domestic jobs.

Many countries have introduced **digital nomad visas** to attract high-spending remote workers and stimulate tourism and local businesses.

Several international rankings, such as the [Global Citizen Solutions Digital Nomad Visa Index](https://www.globalcitizensolutions.com/digital-nomad-visa-index/), compare countries based on visa accessibility, cost of living, and overall quality of life.

## Top digital nomad visas explained

Below are some of the most attractive digital nomad visas currently available.

### Portugal digital nomad visa

Portugal has become one of the most popular destinations for remote workers.

Key features:

- 1-year visa with renewal options
- pathway to residency
- strong coworking and startup ecosystem

Cities like Lisbon, Porto, and Madeira host thriving digital nomad communities. Official requirements and application procedures can be verified on the [Portuguese immigration authority website](https://imigrante.sef.pt/en/).

### Spain digital nomad visa

Spain introduced its digital nomad visa as part of the **Startup Act**.

Key benefits include:

- up to 5 years of residence
- relatively accessible income requirements
- excellent infrastructure and lifestyle

Spain is especially popular among European remote workers. Details about the visa are available on the [Spanish government immigration portal](https://www.inclusion.gob.es/en/web/migraciones).

### Thailand long-term resident visa

Thailand offers long-term residence options designed for remote professionals and entrepreneurs.

The country has long been a hub for digital nomads thanks to destinations like Chiang Mai and Bangkok.

If you're considering Thailand, read our [complete guide to how to get a digital nomad visa in Thailand](/en/blog/how-to-get-a-digital-nomad-visa-in-thailand-in-2025/).

### Indonesia remote worker visa

Indonesia is another top destination for digital nomads, particularly in Bali.

While visa regulations have evolved over the years, Indonesia continues exploring long-term remote work permits.

For a detailed breakdown, see our [guide on how to get a digital nomad visa in Indonesia](/en/blog/how-to-get-a-digital-nomad-visa-in-indonesia-a-complete-guid/).

### Estonia digital nomad visa

Estonia was one of the first countries to introduce a visa specifically designed for digital nomads.

Advantages:

- straightforward application process
- advanced digital infrastructure
- strong tech ecosystem

Estonia was one of the first countries to launch a dedicated digital nomad visa. Full details are available on the [Estonian Police and Border Guard website](https://www.politsei.ee/en).

### Dubai remote work visa

Dubai offers a one-year renewable visa for remote workers.

Highlights:

- tax advantages
- high-end infrastructure
- international connectivity

Dubai also offers a dedicated remote work program that allows professionals to live in the UAE while working for companies abroad. Application requirements and documentation can be verified on the [official Dubai remote work visa page](https://www.visitdubai.com/en/business-in-dubai/remote-working).

However, the cost of living is significantly higher than in many other destinations.

### Croatia digital nomad visa

Croatia has become one of Europe's most attractive remote work destinations.

Benefits include:

- relatively low cost of living
- Mediterranean lifestyle
- growing coworking communities

Croatia has become one of Europe's most attractive destinations for remote workers thanks to its Mediterranean lifestyle and relatively affordable cost of living. Full details about eligibility and application procedures can be found on the [official Croatian government website](https://mup.gov.hr/aliens-281621/stay-and-work/digital-nomads/286833).

## Choosing the right digital nomad visa

Not every visa is suitable for every remote worker.

When choosing a digital nomad visa, consider factors such as:

- minimum income requirements
- visa duration
- cost of living
- internet infrastructure
- community of remote workers

You may also want to compare different destinations before deciding where to apply.

## Quick answers about digital nomad visas

### Which countries offer digital nomad visas?

More than 50 countries now offer digital nomad visa programs, including Portugal, Spain, Estonia, Croatia, and Costa Rica.

### How much income do you need for a digital nomad visa?

Most digital nomad visas require a minimum monthly income between $2,000 and $5,000, depending on the country.

### Are digital nomad visas tax-free?

Not necessarily. Some countries offer tax benefits, but many still require residents to pay local taxes after a certain period.

### What is the easiest digital nomad visa to get?

Countries like Mexico, Costa Rica, and Croatia are often considered among the easiest digital nomad visas due to accessible income requirements.
